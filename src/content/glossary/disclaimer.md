In Exclaimer, **disclaimer** can mean three different things, and the right object depends on the job. The standalone **Disclaimers** feature (Standard and Pro plans) appends plain text up to 5000 characters after any active signature, with its own Senders and Recipients rules and date-window controls; on Gmail it's server-side only. The **Disclaimer signature element** lives inside a signature template and renders Brand Kit text with Brand Kit fonts and colours. Or the disclaimer can simply be a Text element pasted into the template.

Plain-text disclaimer objects can't carry hyperlinks; for hyperlinked legal text use a Text element. Email disclaimers don't provide complete legal protection; the customer's compliance authority owns the text wording, and the MSP implements faithfully. Even typo corrections in regulatory disclosures should be authorised in writing for the audit trail.
