## Why this panel exists

Spaced repetition is one of the highest-evidence interventions in self-paced learning. The `Recall` panel pulls 1-3 questions from a configured pool of previously-completed quizzes and presents them as a quick warmup before today's content.

It's not scored — wrong answers explain themselves and let the learner move on. The point is the act of retrieving from memory, not the grade.

In production, the pool would be filtered against `localStorage.completedLessons` so only questions from quizzes the learner has actually finished surface. This demo always shows the configured pool.

## Two new question types in the final quiz

The end-of-course quiz now also exercises two question types that didn't exist before today.

### Fill in the blank

Per-blank text inputs, case-insensitive + trim. Authors can supply `alternatives` for accepted variants. Useful for record syntax, command flags, exact acronyms — anywhere multiple choice would let the learner pattern-match the wording.

### Label the diagram

Drag-free (mobile-friendly) click-to-place. Bank of labels above an image with marked zones; pick a label, click a zone, the label snaps in. Distractor labels can be added to the pool so the learner has to know which labels belong on the diagram at all.

Both ship as standard quiz types — same `Quiz` + `Checkpoint` components render them. Take the final quiz to see both in action.
