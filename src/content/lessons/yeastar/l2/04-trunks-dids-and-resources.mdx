A new Cloud PBX is born without a way to make or receive PSTN calls. Trunks live one layer up, at the MSP's YCM, and the provisioning team's job is to assign the right trunk and the right DIDs onto each customer's PBX. The same pattern applies to AI minutes: the pool sits at YCM, allocations land on the PBX. This is the lesson where the MSP's shared infrastructure meets the customer's instance.

## The shared-trunk model in two sentences

The MSP holds one or more SIP trunks at YCM. Each customer's Cloud PBX gets the trunks it needs assigned from that pool, with DIDs (inbound numbers) bound to the assignments.

Why this shape: an MSP with 80 customers doesn't want 80 carrier accounts and 80 separate registration credentials to chase. One carrier account, one trunk in YCM, 80 assignments out to customer PBXs. The customer never sees the trunk credentials; they see calls flowing.

## Trunk types

Trunks come in three shapes (the same three any SIP-aware tech sees on any PBX, plus the YCM wrapper):

| Trunk type | When carriers want this | Identifier |
|---|---|---|
| **Register trunk** | The PBX authenticates to the carrier with a username and password | `type: register` |
| **Peer trunk (DID-based)** | The carrier identifies the PBX by the dialled DID, no username | `type: did_based` |
| **Peer trunk (port-based)** | The carrier identifies the PBX by source IP and port | `type: port_based` |

The carrier dictates which one. Read the carrier's onboarding sheet, pick the matching shape, fill the fields. The `voip-sip-in-practice` course's trunk-configuration lesson explains the differences in protocol terms; this lesson is about the YCM-side wrapping.

Trunk creation lives at:

```
Repository  →  Shared Trunks  →  Add
```

## DIDs sit on trunks, not on PBXes

The next mental model that catches techs is that DIDs belong to trunks, not to PBXes. The MSP adds DIDs into YCM and associates them with a trunk:

```
Repository  →  DID Numbers  →  Add
```

A DID record carries:

- `didList`, the numbers themselves (in `+xxx` or `xxx` format, comma-separated for bulk add)
- `didName`, a human label for the batch
- `trunkIds`, the list of trunks the DIDs are assigned to (usually one; the multi-trunk shape exists for failover)

The DID exists in YCM the moment you add it. It isn't yet routed to any customer; that's the next step.

## Binding trunks and DIDs to a Cloud PBX

This is the move most provisioning tickets ask for. The customer has a PBX, the MSP has a carrier trunk and a DID block, and the job is to wire them together.

### Prerequisites

Three things must be true before you start:

- The **shared trunk and the Cloud PBX are in the same region**. A Sydney-hosted trunk cannot be assigned to a Singapore-hosted PBX. If the customer needs both regions, create separate trunks per region.
- The DIDs have been added to that trunk in YCM.
- The Cloud PBX firmware is **84.19.0.22 or later** (the version that introduced shared-trunk DID assignment).

If any of these fails, the **Add** button on the Assigned DIDs tab is greyed or the trunk dropdown is empty.

### The assignment procedure

<StepThrough client:load>
  <Step title="Open the customer's Cloud PBX">
    Cloud PBX list, search the customer, click the row name. The detail page loads.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch to the Assigned DIDs tab">
    The tab list across the top includes Customer, Assigned Trunks, Assigned DIDs, and a few others. Click Assigned DIDs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add">
    A pop-up opens. The first field is **Shared Trunk**, a dropdown of every trunk in the PBX's region.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pick the trunk and the DIDs">
    Pick the trunk; the second field, **DID Numbers**, populates with the DIDs assigned to that trunk in YCM. Pick the DIDs to route to this PBX. Multi-select if the customer gets a number block; single-pick if they get one main number.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add custom SIP headers if the trunk requires them">
    On YCM 87.18.0.46+ and PBX 84.22.0.138+, if the shared trunk has custom SIP headers defined (carrier requires `X-CDR-ACCOUNTID` or similar), the Add Header row appears. Pick the header, enter the value. The value must be unique across all Cloud PBXs for the same header on the same trunk; YCM enforces this at save.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    The assignment commits. On the customer's PBX, the trunk now appears in PSE under Inbound → Trunks, marked as a shared trunk.
  </Step>
</StepThrough>

### Two things the assignment changes

On the PBX side, **once a trunk is assigned**, two surfaces shift:

- The trunk appears in PSE under Inbound → Trunks with a **shared** badge. The customer's PBX admin can see it but **cannot edit the centrally-managed fields** (registration credentials, hostname, codec list). YCM owns those.
- Two fields the PBX admin *can* edit on the shared trunk: **Inbound Caller ID Reformatting** (how the PBX normalises the inbound CallerID) and **Outbound Caller ID** (what the PBX presents on outbound; the carrier may override this depending on type-of-service rules). These two are deliberately per-customer.

Lesson 2 of `yeastar-pse-build` covers the PSE-side trunk surface in more detail; what matters here is that the assignment from YCM is what makes the trunk visible at all.

<Callout type="warn" title="Shared trunks are NOT in PBX system backups">
A backup taken from PSE captures everything except the centrally-managed shared trunks. After a restore (from YCM or from a manual backup file), the trunk is gone from the restored PBX; the MSP has to reassign it from YCM. Lesson 6 covers restore mechanics, and this is the wrinkle to remember.
</Callout>

## Trunk access for Hosting Users

If the MSP runs a sub-reseller channel (Hosting Users with their own PBXes and their own customers), Hosting Users don't see the MSP's trunks by default. The MSP grants per-trunk access from:

```
Users  →  pick the Hosting User  →  Trunk Access Control
```

The grant carries the trunk plus the list of DIDs that Hosting User is allowed to assign onto their customers' PBXes. Without a grant, the trunk dropdown on the Hosting User's create form is empty.

The MSP-direct customer model (no Hosting Users) doesn't need any of this; the Super Admin and the permitted Colleagues see every trunk.

## AI minutes and recording, the three-pool story

The same shared-resource pattern applies to AI transcription minutes, AI receptionist minutes, and recording minutes. There are three pools per minute type per PBX:

| Pool | Field name | Refresh | Use when |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Plan-included** | `transcriptionCapacityPlan` / `receptionistCapacityPlan` | Once, at PBX create | Default; this is what the plan includes |
| **One-time add-on** | `transcriptionCapacityAddon` / `receptionistCapacityAddon` | Once, allocated by MSP from pool | Customer needs more than plan included; one-shot top-up |
| **Monthly pack** | `transcriptionPack` / `receptionistPack` | Refills every month | Customer's sustained usage exceeds plan; ongoing allocation |

Practical rules:

- Recording minutes have the same shape. Each PBX gets 500 free, the MSP can allocate extra from the pool (one-time), and any minutes allocated past the free 500 carry an expiration date (`recordingExpireDate`).
- The customer consumes from plan-included first, then add-on, then pack. The PBX shows the breakdown on the detail page's Capacity panel.
- One-time add-on minutes once issued **cannot be reduced** (same rule as Hosting User recording allocation). Monthly packs can be turned off or downsized at next-month boundary.

### Worked example: Able Moose AI usage

Able Moose's IT contact wants Linkus voicemail-to-text on for the finance team only. Volume estimate is 800 minutes of transcription per month.

Decision tree:

- Plan-included (120 mins for Enterprise) is not enough.
- One-time add-on would top them up for one month; they'd run out the next.
- Monthly pack at 1,000 minutes (one of the supported pack sizes) covers the demand with 200-minute headroom.

The MSP allocates the 1,000-minute monthly pack from the My Subscription pack pool to Able Moose's PBX. The pack ticks down through the month and refills on the billing anniversary. Finance, no recurring tickets, predictable customer-side billing.

## Where assignments are visible after the fact

Three places to check what's been assigned where:

| Question | Where |
|---|---|
| "What trunks and DIDs does this PBX have?" | Cloud PBX detail, Assigned Trunks tab + Assigned DIDs tab |
| "Where is this DID assigned?" | Repository → DID Numbers, the row carries `cloudPbxId` |
| "What did this Hosting User have access to?" | Users → user → Trunk Access Control |

Triage-from-YCM is fast here; you read the assignment without descending into PSE at all. Lesson 6 covers when you do need to descend.
